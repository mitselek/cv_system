## Job Description

About your role As a Supervisor, your responsibilities include managing daily production activities in accordance with our quality system.

You will lead your team by example, guiding them toward achieving set goals.

As part of the supervisory team, you will ensure the department's daily operations are safe, efficient, and high-quality, while also contributing to continuous improvement.

You will act as the supervisor for a newly formed team and, when necessary, participate in operational tasks.

It is possible that your team will grow beyond the currently planned five (5) members in the future.

This is a permanent position.

Currently, tablet production at the Espoo plant operates in a interrupted 3-shift system.

With growth, new shift models are being planned to support expanded operations.

We hope you can start as soon as possible.

Your key responsibilities Leading production activities to achieve shift targets in collaboration with stakeholders Managing the well-being, performance, and competence of your approximately 5-person production team, while also participating in hands-on work Communicating production goals to your team and sharing shift updates with stakeholders and colleagues Continuously improving safety, quality, and productivity in line with Lean principles Ensuring initial actions and reporting for deviations, as well as initiating safety observations and improvement proposals What we offer An interesting and challenging work environment that provides a comprehensive view of tablet manufacturing Opportunities to learn something new every day Strong support from close colleagues and stakeholders (e.g., production technology, quality organization, and product development) At Orion Pharma, your work creates true impact and well-being for our customers, patients and society at large.

Our culture of friendliness, respect, mutual appreciation and diversity creates a safe working environment where you can strive for excellence.

We offer a wealth of career paths and development opportunities that support the development of innovative solutions and improving the quality of life.

Please visit our website to find further information about our values and Orion Pharma as an employer https://www.orion.fi/en/careers/orion-as-an-employer/.

Our expectations To succeed in this role, we expect you to have: Previous supervisory experience or suitability and strong motivation to move into a leadership role Ability to work under pressure and strong interpersonal skills Fluent Finnish language skills, as Finnish is our working language and instructions are in Finnish Sufficient English skills, as English is the official language of our group and widely used in communication and training Experience in a production environment and shift work A relevant higher education degree, such as an engineer (BSc), pharmacist, or other suitable qualification Experience with GMP (Good Manufacturing Practice) and cleanrooms is considered an advantage.

Familiarity with various IT systems is also beneficial (e.g., MS Office, SAP S/4Hana, Gurufield, Oracle).

Unit description Orion Pharma's Global Operations covers the entire pharmaceutical manufacturing process, from sourcing of raw materials to the manufacture of products, packaging and delivery to customers.

We are also responsible for the industrialization of new products and product life cycle management.

In Global Operations we have approximately 1,600 professionals working in Production, our external supplier network (Externally Sourced Operations) and Fermion Oy (API manufacturing and marketing).

In addition, the organization includes Supply Chain & Procurement, Technical Operations, Facility Management, EHS (Environment, Health and Safety), CPD&LCM (Commercial Product Development & Product lifecycle management) and Operations Development.

In Espoo tablet manufacturing, we produce treatments for prostate cancer, Parkinson's disease, cardiovascular diseases, and pain.

We work in cleanrooms and manufacture tablet-form medicines.

Our department employs about 70 people in bulk production, tableting, tablet coating, and equipment maintenance.

Process operators also drive forklifts, handle units of about 2 mÂ³, and use various systems and equipment extensively.

For now, work is mainly in an interrupted 3-shift system, meaning three shifts on weekdays.
