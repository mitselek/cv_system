## Revenue Sharing Models

### Model A: Lead Partnership (60/40 split)

**Your role:** Lead contractor, partner as major subcontractor

**You provide:**

- Project Manager (critical role)
- 1 Developer position
- Client relationship management
- Business development

**Partner provides:**

- Reference portfolio (2-3 projects)
- 2 additional developers
- DevOps infrastructure
- Technical backend support

**Revenue split:** You 60% / Partner 40%

**Pros:**

- Your name on contract
- Direct client relationship
- Higher margin

**Cons:**

- Requires your financial qualification (1M EUR turnover)
- You bear primary contracting risk
- More administrative overhead

---

### Model B: Equal Partnership (50/50 split)

**Joint bid:** Both companies as consortium

**You provide:**

- Project Manager + 1 Developer
- Government sector expertise
- Estonian language advantage

**Partner provides:**

- Reference portfolio
- 2-3 Developers
- DevOps infrastructure

**Revenue split:** 50% / 50%

**Pros:**

- Shared risk and responsibility
- Equal decision-making
- Combined financial strength

**Cons:**

- Need legal consortium agreement
- More complex governance
- Requires aligned company cultures

---

### Model C: Subcontractor Model (30/70 split)

**Your role:** Subcontractor to partner's lead bid

**You provide:**

- Project Manager services (your unique strength)
- 1 Developer position
- Consulting and architecture

**Partner provides:**

- Lead contractor role
- Full reference portfolio
- Development team
- All compliance and financial responsibility

**Revenue split:** You 30% / Partner 70%

**Pros:**

- Lower risk for you
- No financial qualification requirement on your side
- Simpler legal structure
- Focus on your strengths (PM, development)

**Cons:**

- Lower total revenue
- No direct client relationship
- Dependent on partner's contract management
- Limited business development opportunity
