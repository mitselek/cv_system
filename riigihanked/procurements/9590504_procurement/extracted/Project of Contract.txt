referred to as the Party and jointly as the Parties, entered into a
procurement „….“ reference number …. as a result of the provisions of
this Contract (hereinafter referred to as the Contract):

1.  Contract documents

1.1. The Contract documents comprise the Contract, the procurement
documents, the Contractor's tender (hereinafter the Tender) and
amendments to the Contract, which shall be agreed upon, if necessary,
after the conclusion of the Contract.

1.2. In the event of a conflict between the Contract and its annexes,
the Parties shall be guided primarily by the procurement documents.

2.  Object and duration of the Contract

2.1. The object of the Contract is…. (hereinafter together or separately
the Service) in accordance with the procurement documents.

2.2. The Contract shall enter into force upon conclusion and shall be
valid for …. Months / YYYY-MM-DD.

3.  Contract fee and payment terms

    1.  The price of the Contract is ……. euros. VAT is added to this
        amount.

    2.  The price of the Service includes all expenses of the Contractor
        concerning the provision of the Service.

    3.  The Contracting Authority shall make advance payments if needed.

    4.  All payments arising from the Contract shall be made in euros.

4.  Liability of the Parties

    1.  The Parties shall be fully liable for the direct property damage
        caused to the other Party by the non-performance or unacceptable
        performance of the obligations arising from the Contract. The
        total liability of a Party is limited to the contract fee,
        unless the breach of Contract was intentional.

    2.  In case of non-observance of the term specified on the basis of
        the Contract, the Contracting Authority has the right to demand
        a contractual penalty from the Contractor of up to 0.05% (zero
        point zero five percent) of the price of the Contract for each
        day of delay.

    3.  If the Contracting Authority delays fulfilling the financial
        obligations provided in the Contract, the Contractor has the
        right to demand from the Contracting Authority interest at the
        rate of 0.05% (zero point zero five percent) of the unpaid
        amount per day, but not more than 5% (five percent) of the order
        cost.

    4.  In the event of non-performance or unacceptable performance of
        the obligations provided for in the Contract, if it can be
        considered a material breach of the Contract, the Contracting
        Authority has the right to terminate the Contract unilaterally
        by notifying the Contractor in writing. Violation of the
        Contract is considered significant primarily in the
        circumstances described in § 116 (2) of the Estonian Law of
        Obligations Act.

    5.  Penalties and default interest must be paid within 14 (fourteen)
        days of receiving the respective claim. The Contracting
        Authority has the right to set off the amount of the contractual
        penalty with the amount payable under the Contract upon payment
        for the object.

    6.  Non-performance or unacceptable performance of the obligations
        arising from the Contract shall not be deemed a breach of the
        Contract if it was caused by force majeure. If the force majeure
        lasts for more than 90 days, the Contract shall be deemed
        terminated with impossibility to perform. In such a case, the
        Parties shall not have the right to demand compensation for
        damage caused by non-performance or unacceptable performance of
        the Contract.

5.  Amendment and early termination of the Contract

    1.  By agreement of the Parties, the Contract may be amended or
        extraordinarily terminated before the date specified in Clause
        2.2. The Contract shall be amended or terminated by written
        agreement of the Parties.

    2.  Early termination of the Contract may occur unilaterally on the
        following grounds, notifying the other Party in writing 60
        (sixty) calendar days in advance:

        1.  The Contracting Authority may terminate the Contract early
            if the Contractor violates the Contract or repeatedly fails
            to perform the obligations specified in the Contract or
            repeatedly fails to perform the obligations according to the
            terms of the Contract;

        2.  The Contractor may terminate the Contract early if the
            Contracting Authority materially violates the Contract or
            has repeatedly failed to pay the invoices on time.

6.  Notices and contact persons

    1.  Notices between the Parties relating to the Contract shall be
        submitted in a form that can be reproduced in writing, except in
        cases where such notices are of an informational nature and have
        no legal consequences to the Parties. Notices for information
        may also be given orally.

    2.  A notice is considered delivered if it has been sent by e-mail
        or registered mail and 3 (three) days have passed since the
        posting.

    3.  The Contact Persons in matters related to the performance of the
        Contract are:

        1.  Contracting Authority: __________________________

        2.  Contractor: ______________________________

    4.  The Parties are obliged to notify the other Party immediately of
        any change in the legal addresses, responsible persons,
        authorised representatives and / or contact details of the
        Parties.

7.  Final provisions

    1.  The Parties may not transfer the obligations arising from the
        Contract to third parties without the prior written consent of
        the Parties.

    2.  Disputes between the Parties arising from the Contract shall be
        settled by negotiations between the Parties. If no agreement is
        reached, disputes will be settled in court.

    3.  In issues not regulated by the Contract, the Parties will be
        governed by legislative acts of the Republic of Estonia.

    4.  With this Contract the Parties confirm that they have no
        obstacles to the conclusion and performance of the Contract and
        that the Parties do not violate the interests of third parties
        by concluding the Contract.

    5.  The Parties sign the Contract digitally. / The contract is
        compiled in two identical copies having identical and equal
        legal force, one copy to each party.
